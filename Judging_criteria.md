Project Details
Problem Statement
Most citizens want to understand what their elected leaders are doing in parliament but the reality is that parliamentary information is structurally inaccessible to ordinary people. Not everyone can sit for hours watching live parliamentary sessions. Not everyone has the time or energy to read through hundreds of pages of bills written in complex legal language. Most people simply want to know: “What did my representative say, and how does it affect me?”. Yet today’s parliamentary communication system makes that almost impossible. Citizens struggle because: • Live sittings happen during working hours, when most people are at school, business, or employment. • Sessions are extremely long, often running for hours, making it unrealistic for youths, professionals, or parents to follow them. • Videos and live-streams are hard to navigate, forcing people to scrub manually to find a specific topic or MP’s speech. • Hansard documents are massive PDFs, difficult to search and written in technical parliamentary jargon. • Tracking a representative’s history, stance, or consistency over months or years is practically impossible without doing manual research. • Hidden clauses and unclear amendments in large bills make it easy for critical details to be overlooked by the public. When information is hard to access, misinformation fills the gap, fueling confusion and distrust. This information vacuum was a major factor behind events such as the Gen-Z–led 2024 protests, where citizens demanded transparency, accountability, and clarity about decisions affecting their future. Because accessible political information does not exist, citizens are left feeling unrepresented, unheard, and uninformed.They rely on secondary sources, rumors, influencers and clipped videos instead of direct, trustworthy parliamentary data.This creates a dangerous cycle: Lack of accessibility → lack of understanding → lack of trust → civic frustration and unrest. Citizens want transparency. They want clarity. They want a way to efficiently track what leaders say, how they vote, and what bills contain without needing to be parliamentary experts or full-time analysts. But today, no tool gives them that power.

Proposed Solution
ParliaScope turns hours of parliamentary debates, dense PDF documents, and complex bills into clear, searchable, citizen-friendly information instantly. Instead of forcing people to sit through entire sittings or decode legal jargon, ParliaScope uses AI-powered speech-to-text, semantic search, sentiment analysis, and speaker tracking to break down parliamentary proceedings into simple, meaningful insights that anyone can understand.It transforms opaque parliamentary content into publicly accessible, interactive political knowledge closing the information gap between government actions and the people they serve. With ParliaScope, citizens can: • Search any topic from “affordable housing” to “tax reforms” across thousands of speeches in seconds. • See what their representative actually said, summarised and sentiment-classified for quick understanding. • Track a leader’s consistency over months or years, revealing patterns in their positions and priorities. • Navigate any Hansard or sitting efficiently, finding specific discussions without watching hours of video or reading hundreds of pages. • Access bill highlights and potential impacts in plain language, reducing the chance of hidden clauses being overlooked. • Receive alerts when their MP debates key issues, making political engagement effortless and continuous. By giving citizens, a direct, intelligent, and transparent window into parliamentary activity, ParliaScope empowers informed civic participation, strengthens democratic accountability, and prevents the confusion and frustration that have historically led to public unrest. This is not just a tool. It is a new standard for political transparency built for the modern citizen.

Relevance to Theme
ParliaScope directly supports Kenya’s vision for AI-driven national prosperity by making governance more transparent, accessible, and citizen-centered. Many Kenyans cannot follow parliamentary debates due to work, school, or limited airtime access, leading to a disconnect between citizens and their representatives. This gap contributes to mistrust, misinformation, and civic frustration — issues seen clearly during movements like the 2024 Gen-Z protests. ParliaScope uses AI to convert long, complex parliamentary content into clear, timely insights that every citizen can understand. By simplifying bills, summarizing debates, and highlighting patterns in leadership behavior, the platform strengthens accountability while empowering people to track decisions that affect their daily lives. This promotes stability, reduces corruption-related risks, and enables informed dialogue between citizens and policymakers. In doing so, ParliaScope enhances good governance — a foundation for sustainable development, national security, and economic growth. Furthermore, by making political information transparent and easily searchable, ParliaScope supports evidence-based decision-making for both leaders and the public. Investors, SMEs, educators, and researchers benefit from clear interpretations of legislation, improving economic predictability and trust. The platform aligns with Kenya’s broader AI strategy by demonstrating how machine learning can strengthen public institutions without replacing human decision-making. Ultimately, ParliaScope encourages a culture of openness, civic education, and responsible AI adoption — all essential for a prosperous and secure nation.
